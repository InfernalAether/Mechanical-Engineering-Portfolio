About Me

Hello! My name is Andrew Huang. I graduated Chaffey College for my Associate's in Engineering back in May of 2025. I am now pursuing my Bachelor's Degree in Mechanical Engineering at San Francisco State University, with the specialty of robotics. This is my portfolio of my projects and experiences as an engineer.

This page is a little about me. Growing up, I never really understood or knew what I wanted to do when I grew up. I kind of didn't have it figured out, even when I was applying for colleges. I first went through the idea of getting my medical degree, in hopes to become a surgeon. It wasn't until I was about to begin community college that I realized that I wanted to be an engineer. During my primary education years, I was very interested in the math and science subjects versus any other, almost always excelling in this classes. I realized that I love to do anything that relates to how these two connect with each other, which brought me to realization that physics was definitely one of the major science classes that I had fun in. I remember always wanting to fix things that were broken or build things out of whatever I can find in the household, which was mostly legos by the way, and I came to the realization that I can take this passion and turn it into a job, which is engineering. As we develop technologically, I realized that I always wanted to understand these technological advancements and be a part of them, so I decided that I was going to focus on robotics. Engineering classes aren't always so easy for me but I do love them and enjoy seeing how the world really works with engineering.

Okay, this is a little more about me if you want to get to know me better! I was born in Monterey Park, California. I am a first-generation Chinese-American, born to immigrant parents. I never really knew my parents as they divorced back when I was just a baby and went their separate ways. I was with my dad until I was 8, where he would leave for China and never really come back, so I was left with the care of my aunt and uncle. Growing up, I worked with them in their little stall at the Bel-Air Swap-meet in Bloomington, CA. I basically lived in SoCal my entire life, up until now, and I grew up in a different environment that was NorCal has to offer. I did a few hobbies as a kid, some which I am still interested today, which includes playing the violin, playing and learning tennis, and gaming. Boy, am I a gamer? I loved gaming, it has been a huge part of my life, and introduced me to many people around the world that I was able to call friends and learn from. Tennis has been a huge part of my high school career, as I was varsity tennis my sophomore year and I made a lot of friends and memories because of it. Growing up, I kind of had an unevenful life as my family didn't really travel much and did much, so I just mostly stayed at home or went out with friends. I would eventually try to find other jobs though in order to start making some money for myself, which landed me a job at Michael's. This job didn't really last long as I needed to transfer schools the following month to San Francisco State University. Currently, I am hoping to make more connections here and adapt to the Bay Area lifestyle.
